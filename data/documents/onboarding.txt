Employee Onboarding Policy Document

1. Overview

The onboarding process is designed to ensure a smooth transition of new employees into the organization. It includes documentation, system access, orientation, and role alignment.

--------------------------------------------------

2. Pre-Onboarding Process

2.1 Offer Letter:
Selected candidates receive an official offer letter via email containing:
- Job role
- Salary structure
- Joining date
- Terms and conditions

2.2 Offer Acceptance:
Candidates must accept the offer within the specified deadline.

2.3 Background Verification:
HR conducts background verification including:
- Educational qualification check
- Previous employment verification
- Identity verification

--------------------------------------------------

3. Document Submission

3.1 Mandatory Documents:
Employees must submit the following documents:
- Government ID proof (Aadhar/PAN/Passport)
- Address proof
- Educational certificates
- Previous employment documents (if applicable)
- Passport size photographs

3.2 Submission Mode:
Documents must be uploaded via the HR portal before joining date.

--------------------------------------------------

4. Day 1 Joining Process

4.1 Welcome Session:
New employees attend a welcome session conducted by HR.

4.2 HR Introduction:
Overview of company policies, work culture, and expectations.

4.3 Team Introduction:
Employees are introduced to their team and reporting manager.

--------------------------------------------------

5. IT Setup and Access

5.1 Email Creation:
Official company email ID is created.

5.2 System Access:
Access is provided to:
- Internal systems
- HR portal
- Communication tools (Slack, Teams, etc.)

5.3 Hardware Allocation:
Laptop/desktop and required accessories are issued.

--------------------------------------------------

6. Orientation Program

6.1 Orientation includes:
- Company overview
- Organizational structure
- Policies and compliance
- Code of conduct

6.2 Duration:
Orientation typically lasts 1–2 days.

--------------------------------------------------

7. Role-Based Training

7.1 Employees undergo training specific to their job role.

7.2 Training includes:
- Technical skills
- Tools and software usage
- Process training

7.3 Training duration varies depending on role.

--------------------------------------------------

8. Probation Period

8.1 Duration:
Standard probation period is 6 months.

8.2 Performance Evaluation:
Employees are evaluated based on:
- Work quality
- Attendance
- Team collaboration

8.3 Confirmation:
Successful employees are confirmed as permanent staff after probation.

--------------------------------------------------

9. Code of Conduct Agreement

9.1 Employees must agree to company policies including:
- Confidentiality
- Data security
- Workplace behavior

--------------------------------------------------

10. Exit During Onboarding

10.1 If an employee leaves during onboarding:
- Access will be revoked immediately
- Final settlement will be processed as per policy

--------------------------------------------------

11. HR Support

11.1 Employees can contact HR for:
- Queries related to policies
- System access issues
- Documentation help

--------------------------------------------------

End of Document
