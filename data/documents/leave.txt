HR Leave Policy Document

1. Types of Leave

1.1 Casual Leave (CL):
Employees are entitled to 12 days of casual leave per calendar year. Casual leave is intended for short-term personal needs such as urgent work, personal commitments, or emergencies. Casual leave cannot be carried forward to the next year.

1.2 Sick Leave (SL):
Employees are entitled to 10 days of sick leave annually. Sick leave can be availed in case of illness or medical emergencies. For leaves exceeding 2 consecutive days, a medical certificate must be submitted.

1.3 Earned Leave (EL):
Employees earn 1 day of earned leave for every 20 working days completed. Earned leave can be accumulated and carried forward to the next year, subject to a maximum limit of 15 days.

1.4 Maternity Leave:
Female employees are entitled to 26 weeks of paid maternity leave as per company policy and applicable labor laws.

1.5 Paternity Leave:
Male employees are entitled to 5 days of paternity leave during childbirth.

1.6 Loss of Pay (LOP):
If an employee has exhausted all leave balances, additional leave taken will be treated as Loss of Pay.

--------------------------------------------------

2. Leave Application Process

2.1 Employees must apply for leave through the HR portal.

2.2 Leave requests should be submitted at least:
- 2 days in advance for casual leave
- 7 days in advance for planned earned leave

2.3 Emergency leave should be informed to the reporting manager as soon as possible.

2.4 All leave requests must be approved by the reporting manager.

--------------------------------------------------

3. Leave Approval Rules

3.1 Approval depends on:
- Work requirements
- Team availability
- Project deadlines

3.2 Managers have the right to reject or reschedule leave based on business needs.

--------------------------------------------------

4. Leave Carry Forward Policy

4.1 Casual Leave:
Cannot be carried forward.

4.2 Sick Leave:
Cannot be carried forward.

4.3 Earned Leave:
Can be carried forward up to a maximum of 15 days.

--------------------------------------------------

5. Leave Encashment

5.1 Earned leave can be encashed at the end of the year or during resignation.

5.2 Encashment is calculated based on basic salary.

--------------------------------------------------

6. Public Holidays

6.1 Employees are entitled to 12 paid public holidays per year.

6.2 Holiday list is published at the beginning of the year.

--------------------------------------------------

7. Unauthorized Leave

7.1 Absence without approval will be treated as unauthorized leave.

7.2 Salary deduction may apply.

7.3 Repeated violations may lead to disciplinary action.

--------------------------------------------------

8. Probation Period Leave Rules

8.1 Employees on probation are entitled to limited leave.

8.2 Excess leave during probation may extend probation period.

--------------------------------------------------

9. Special Leave

9.1 Bereavement Leave:
3 days leave in case of death of immediate family member.

9.2 Marriage Leave:
5 days leave granted for employee’s marriage.

--------------------------------------------------

10. Work From Home as Leave Alternative

10.1 In certain cases, employees may request Work From Home instead of leave.

10.2 Approval is subject to manager discretion.

--------------------------------------------------
